# Hypoxia-inducible factor 1 protects neurons from Sarm1-mediated neurodegeneration

**Cell Reports** (2026) | [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.celrep.2025.116873)

## Key Claims
- Loss of VHL suppresses SARM1-mediated cellular degeneration (found via CRISPR-Cas9 screen)
- VHL promotes O2-dependent constitutive ubiquitination and degradation of HIF-1; during hypoxia, HIF-1 is stabilized
- Depletion of VHL or other factors required for HIF-1 degradation is neuroprotective in primary neurons
- Expression of a non-ubiquitinated HIF-1 variant strongly blocks axon degeneration in primary mouse neurons and Drosophila
- Hypoxia inhibits SARM1 activity through HIF-1-driven transcriptional changes

## Relevance to Our Work
- **Demonstrates VHL E3 ligase is present and functional in mouse DRG neurons** (shown via CRISPR-Cas9 knockdown affecting HIF-1α/SARM1 pathway)
- This confirms VHL can be leveraged for the dTAG system using dTAG-V1 PROTAC in DRG neurons
