# VHL in DRG Neurons and the dTAG System

## Key Insight

VHL E3 ligase is present and functional in mouse dorsal root ganglion neurons. This was demonstrated in a recent Cell Reports paper where CRISPR-Cas9 knockdown of VHL affected the HIF-1α/SARM1 pathway in these neurons.[^1] While the paper's focus is on neuroprotection via HIF-1 stabilization, the critical takeaway for our purposes is different: **VHL expression in mouse DRG neurons means the dTAG-V1 PROTAC system can be used there**, since dTAG-V1 recruits VHL as its E3 ligase for targeted protein degradation.[^2]

## Implications
- Validates VHL as a usable E3 ligase handle in DRG neurons for PROTAC-based approaches
- Supports feasibility of the dTAG system in sensory neuron experiments
